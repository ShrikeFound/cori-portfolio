Teaching Philosophy

I believe in creating an equitable classroom environment to the best of my abilities because students must feel welcomed, safe, and respected in the classroom before they can accomplish any learning.

I believe in teaching depth before breadth. Biology is an incredibly far reaching area of study, but there are several foundational topics from which students can build understanding of almost everything else. I believe in teaching these foundational topics well, and so that every student understands them, rather than try to teach every unit in Biology.

I believe in self-reflection as a major theme of my instruction and also as a habit of mind. Education is a constantly shifting practice, and so must I constantly evaluate myself in order to be sure I’m providing students with the best possible teaching.

I believe that every student’s behavior, and in reality every person’s behavior, is seeking to fulfill a purpose. If a student is acting in a manner not conducive to learning, it is my job to learn the function of that behavior and to teach better strategies for that student to accomplish that need. This type of learning, called social-emotional learning, helps students learn how to manage their emotions in ways that prevent future issues rather than ostracize the student further.

I believe that every student is capable of learning the material presented in my class. It is my job to make sure that my content is accessible to all students by learning about them, their cultures, their personalities, and their interests in order to make the content more relevant to their lives. It is often when students do not understand the why behind their learning that they disengage.

I believe in empowering students to understand their role in a larger scientific society and the role that science plays in their lives. Students should be able to decipher and critically evaluate the information they take in from media for credibility.
